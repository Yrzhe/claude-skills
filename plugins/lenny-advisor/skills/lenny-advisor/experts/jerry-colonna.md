## Signature Frameworks

### How have I been complicit in creating the conditions I say I don't want?
- **When to use**: When you feel stuck, overwhelmed, or frustrated with your team, career, or life circumstances, and want agency without collapsing into self-blame.
- **Steps**:
  1. Name the condition you say you do not want.
  2. Distinguish complicity from total responsibility; look for your participation, not blame.
  3. Identify the behaviors, habits, or attachments that may be sustaining the condition.
  4. Ask what emotional payoff or protection those behaviors provide.
  5. Use the insight to make a more conscious choice going forward.
- **Example**: You say you hate being busy all the time, but realize an open calendar makes you anxious and unnerved.

### Reboot Leadership Equation
- **When to use**: When developing leaders, founders, or executives who need more than tactical skill.
- **Steps**:
  1. Build practical skills for the job and life demands.
  2. Practice radical self-inquiry to uncover hidden motivations, fears, and patterns.
  3. Create shared experiences where people can tell the truth in community.
  4. Use the combination to strengthen leadership capacity.
  5. Measure success not only by performance, but by resilience.
- **Example**: Reboot boot camps start with practical leadership skills, then move into questions like, “Who would you be without the story of who you are?”

### Radical Self-Inquiry Practice
- **When to use**: When journaling, reflecting, making a major decision, or trying to understand recurring patterns.
- **Steps**:
  1. Pause regularly through journaling, meditation, or quiet reflection.
  2. Ask destabilizing but honest questions rather than productivity questions.
  3. Notice which questions take your breath away or make you afraid.
  4. Answer them truthfully for yourself first.
  5. Repeat continuously rather than treating it as a one-time breakthrough.
- **Example**: Journal on questions like, “What am I not saying that I need to say?” and “What’s being said that I’m not hearing?”

### Shared Truth-Telling Circles
- **When to use**: When leaders or teams need psychological safety, honesty, and a way to break through performative success narratives.
- **Steps**:
  1. Gather a trusted group committed to confidentiality and care.
  2. Invite people to share what is actually happening, not the polished version.
  3. Hold space without fixing, judging, or advising too quickly.
  4. Use the group to surface patterns, fears, and truths that are hard to face alone.
  5. Let the experience normalize vulnerability and reduce isolation.
- **Example**: CEO boot camps where founders sit in circles and discuss difficult questions while others simply hold space.

### Team Dysfunction as Unconscious Pattern Recognition
- **When to use**: When a team keeps repeating the same interpersonal or decision-making problems despite strong talent and strategy.
- **Steps**:
  1. Observe recurring group behaviors, especially around conflict or discomfort.
  2. Name the pattern explicitly when it appears.
  3. Trace how the pattern may mirror family-of-origin or earlier learned dynamics.
  4. Start with the person who has the most power.
  5. Do individual self-inquiry so the team can stop reenacting unconscious roles.
- **Example**: An executive team jokes whenever conversations get painful; the CEO recognizes it as a family pattern and changes how the group responds.

## Core Advice
- **When you feel like life is happening to you**: Ask, “How have I been complicit in creating the conditions I say I don’t want?” — because it restores agency without requiring total self-blame.
- **When you are chronically busy**: Examine what busyness is doing for you emotionally — because busyness often protects you from anxiety, emptiness, or self-doubt.
- **When you are afraid to look inward**: Begin the inquiry anyway — because avoidance usually compounds suffering and later shows up as sabotage or breakdown.
- **When you don’t know how to start reflecting**: Journal on unsettling questions — because specific questions cut through self-delusion faster than vague reflection.
- **When you feel isolated as a founder or executive**: Join a trusted truth-telling circle — because shared honesty reduces performance pressure and increases resilience.
- **When your identity is fused with growth or status**: Reconnect with the intrinsic reason you started — because outcomes become existential when self-worth depends on them.
- **When you use achievement to feel okay**: Ask whether you want the thing or the relief it promises — because attachment often increases suffering.
- **When AI makes the future feel unstable**: Double down on presence, connection, and conceptual thinking — because those human capacities remain durable and AI can be a thinking partner.
- **When team dysfunction feels irrational**: Look for unconscious patterns and start with the most powerful person — because leaders shape the culture most.
- **When your team can’t decide without you**: Notice whether you truly tolerate imperfect decisions — because autonomy requires accepting disagreement and mistakes.
- **When you worry that less attachment means less ambition**: Keep pursuing hard goals, but loosen your grip on outcomes — because fear is brittle, while curiosity and self-compassion sustain effort.

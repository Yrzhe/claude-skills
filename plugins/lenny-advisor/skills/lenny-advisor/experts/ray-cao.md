## Signature Frameworks

### Context, No Control
- **When to use**: When building a high-autonomy team that needs to move quickly without heavy top-down management.
- **Steps**:
  1. Give people full context on the business problem, market, and goals.
  2. Encourage team members to think like business owners rather than task executors.
  3. Let people propose solutions instead of prescribing exact instructions.
  4. Require proactive thinking before action.
  5. Use collaboration to align and execute once the right direction is identified.
- **Example**: At TikTok, teams get context and are trusted to figure out the best path, rather than being told exactly what to do.

### Globalization by Local Immersion
- **When to use**: When launching or scaling a product across multiple countries and cultures.
- **Steps**:
  1. Deploy local talent in each market instead of relying only on central teams.
  2. Study local culture and user behavior directly.
  3. Fine-tune the product and algorithm based on local preferences.
  4. Seed the platform with content/use cases that fit the local market.
  5. Use machine learning as a tool, but validate and adjust with human market insight.
- **Example**: TikTok content differs by market: Japan leans toward food, recipes, and consumer electronics, while the US evolved from lip-syncing into shopping and discovery.

### Cross-Functional Operating Cadence
- **When to use**: When product, engineering, sales, and data teams need to stay tightly aligned in a fast-growing company.
- **Steps**:
  1. Run structured recurring meetings with leaders and ICs together.
  2. Use doc-reading formats so everyone shares the same context before discussion.
  3. Surface decisions, blockers, and celebrations in the same forum.
  4. Bring PMs and engineers into customer immersion trips.
  5. Keep feedback loops between market, product, engineering, and sales very short.
- **Example**: TikTok runs large quarterly meetings with about 180 people using a reading format, and also takes PMs and engineers on client immersion trips.

### Test-and-Learn Creative Optimization
- **When to use**: When advertising or creating content on TikTok, where performance depends on rapid iteration and platform-native behavior.
- **Steps**:
  1. Create many creative variations instead of relying on one asset.
  2. Test broadly before narrowing targeting.
  3. Use platform tools like Creative Hub, analytics, and CapCut to speed production.
  4. Watch trends and adapt creative to what people are already engaging with.
  5. Iterate based on performance data and platform feedback.
- **Example**: Ray recommends at least 10 ad creatives per week for advertisers to learn what works on TikTok.

### Full-Funnel Discovery Marketing
- **When to use**: When evaluating TikTok as an advertising channel for both awareness and conversion.
- **Steps**:
  1. Treat TikTok as a discovery engine, not just an awareness channel.
  2. Build organic presence first to understand the platform.
  3. Run ads to validate impact on traffic, actions, and commerce.
  4. Measure downstream business outcomes, not just views.
  5. Use TikTok Shop, shop ads, and commerce formats when relevant.
- **Example**: The “TikTok made me buy it” phenomenon shows TikTok drives actions, not just brand awareness.

## Core Advice
- **When you want your team to move fast without becoming chaotic**: Give broad context, then let people think and act like owners rather than assigning narrow tasks — because people make better decisions when they understand the full puzzle and can connect the dots across functions.
- **When you are hiring for a high-autonomy, high-speed environment**: Prioritize curiosity, discipline, and prioritization ability over raw output or speed alone — because autonomy only works when people are naturally curious, rigorous, and able to choose the right work.
- **When you are expanding into a new country or region**: Invest in local talent and local market research instead of assuming the same playbook will work everywhere — because culture shapes behavior, and behavior determines what product or content will resonate.
- **When a product seems like it should work globally because of the algorithm**: Fine-tune the product with human insight; don’t rely on the machine alone — because algorithms help, but local teams are needed to interpret culture and adjust to real user behavior.
- **When you need product, engineering, and sales to stay aligned**: Use shared docs and large cross-functional review meetings so everyone starts from the same context — because alignment breaks down when teams operate in silos or only leaders share information.
- **When your team is building for customers but feels too far from the market**: Take PMs and engineers into customer immersion trips so they can see the pain firsthand — because first-hand exposure creates better product judgment and stronger go-to-market decisions.
- **When you are scaling a team quickly**: Do not sacrifice hiring quality for speed — because bad hires slow the team down more than a temporary staffing gap does.
- **When your org structure starts to limit growth**: Reorganize teams to match market needs and growth goals, even if it means breaking existing seams — because rigid structures can prevent the company from moving at the pace the market requires.
- **When you are planning OKRs**: Use OKRs as a cross-functional alignment tool, not a siloed checklist — because the value of OKRs comes from connecting inputs, outputs, and dependencies across teams.
- **When you are launching ads on TikTok for the first time**: Start broad, test many creatives, and give it at least a month to learn before judging performance — because TikTok is a content graph, so early success comes from experimentation and platform-specific learning.
- **When you are choosing targeting on TikTok**: Avoid over-narrow remarketing or niche targeting at the start; begin with broader targeting — because broad targeting helps the system learn and gives you a better read on what resonates.
- **When you are creating content for TikTok**: Be unfiltered, authentic, and willing to iterate on what you post — because TikTok rewards originality and content people want to see, not perfection or polish alone.

- **Conventional**: Hiring fast is the right answer when a business is growing quickly. → **Their view**: Hiring the wrong people to keep up with growth is worse than being temporarily understaffed. — because low-quality hires create drag and slow the organization down more than the original staffing shortage.
- **Conventional**: TikTok is mainly an upper-funnel awareness channel. → **Their view**: TikTok can drive full-funnel outcomes, including actions and commerce. — because Ray points to commerce behavior and the “TikTok made me buy it” trend as evidence of downstream conversion impact.
- **Conventional**: Advertisers should immediately narrow targeting to be efficient. → **Their view**: On TikTok, broad targeting and more creative testing are better early on. — because the platform learns through exploration, and over-targeting limits discovery.

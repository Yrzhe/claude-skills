# Ethan Smith

**CEO** | **Graphite** | Expertise: SEO, answer-engine-optimization, go-to-market, content strategy

## Bio
Ethan Smith is the CEO of Graphite and a longtime SEO operator who has worked in search since 2007. His perspective matters because he applies rigorous experimentation and classic search principles to the emerging world of Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), helping companies understand how to show up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and other LLM-driven answer surfaces.

He is especially valuable for teams that want a practical, testable playbook rather than hype. Ethan’s view is that AEO is not magic—it is search plus summarization, with new rules around citations, long-tail questions, and offsite presence.

## Signature Frameworks

### AEO Optimization Framework
- **When to use**: When you want your product or brand to appear more often in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and other LLM answers.
- **Steps**:
  1. Start from paid search terms, SEO keywords, and competitor money terms.
  2. Convert those keywords into natural-language questions users would ask in LLMs.
  3. Track those questions across multiple LLM surfaces and prompt variants.
  4. Analyze which citations appear in answers and group them by type.
  5. Create or improve landing pages based on the page types that already win.
  6. Answer the main question plus follow-ups, subtopics, use cases, integrations, and feature details.
  7. Run offsite citation strategies across YouTube, Reddit, affiliates, and trusted sources.
  8. Validate tactics with controlled experiments.
- **Example**: For Webflow, Ethan combined SEO landing pages with YouTube/video efforts and Reddit optimization to increase visibility in answer engines.

### Head vs. Tail Model for AEO
- **When to use**: When adapting traditional SEO thinking to LLM-based answer engines.
- **Steps**:
  1. Treat the “head” as citation frequency, not just ranking your own page first.
  2. Increase mentions across trusted sources so the model sees your brand repeatedly.
  3. Treat the “tail” as much larger than in search.
  4. Create content for specific follow-up questions and obscure use cases.
  5. Mine sales calls, support tickets, Reddit, and customer conversations for long-tail questions.
- **Example**: A new YC startup can show up quickly if it gets cited in a Reddit thread, YouTube video, or blog post, even without traditional domain authority.

### Citation Optimization Framework
- **When to use**: When your goal is to improve offsite presence in the sources LLMs cite.
- **Steps**:
  1. Audit which citation sources appear most often for your target prompts.
  2. Segment opportunities into your site, video platforms, UGC communities, affiliates, and blogs.
  3. Prioritize channels that are both trusted and influenceable.
  4. Participate transparently and usefully in communities.
  5. Invest in paid affiliate placements where it makes economic sense.
  6. Measure which citation types increase share of voice.
- **Example**: Ethan noted that YouTube/Vimeo are relatively easy to influence, Reddit is high-impact but community-policed, and affiliate list placements are expensive but controllable.

### Reddit Optimization Method
- **When to use**: When Reddit is frequently cited for your category and you want authentic brand presence there.
- **Steps**:
  1. Find Reddit threads that already rank or are likely to be cited.
  2. Use real employee accounts.
  3. Disclose who you are and where you work.
  4. Add genuinely useful information.
  5. Avoid automation, fake accounts, vote manipulation, and spam.
  6. Focus on a small number of high-quality contributions.
- **Example**: At Webflow, team members replied in relevant threads by stating their name, where they worked, and then offering useful information.

### AEO Experiment Design
- **When to use**: When evaluating whether a tactic like Reddit comments, videos, or new landing pages actually improves answer visibility.
- **Steps**:
  1. Select a set of target questions.
  2. Split them into control and test groups.
  3. Leave the control group untouched.
  4. Apply one intervention to the test group.
  5. Track answer visibility for a few weeks before and after.
  6. Compare movement against the control group.
  7. Repeat to confirm reproducibility before scaling.
- **Example**: Take 200 questions, intervene on 100, leave 100 untouched, and compare results because answer visibility naturally fluctuates.

### Help Center Optimization for AEO
- **When to use**: When users ask detailed product capability questions in LLMs and your help center can become the best citation source.
- **Steps**:
  1. Move the help center from a subdomain to a subdirectory if possible.
  2. Improve internal linking.
  3. Expand beyond common head terms into long-tail use cases and integrations.
  4. Mine sales and support conversations for unanswered questions.
  5. Create pages for obscure but real scenarios.
  6. Consider community-generated Q&A for the long tail.
- **Example**: A user asks which meeting transcription tool integrates with Looker; the real answer may involve Otter, Zapier, and BigQuery, but no help article exists yet.

### Information Gain Heuristic
- **When to use**: When creating content that should stand out in search and answer engines instead of blending into generic SEO content.
- **Steps**:
  1. Ask whether your content says something others do not.
  2. Avoid rewriting what already ranks.
  3. Add original research, expert insight, or firsthand knowledge.
  4. Check whether the content feels derivative.
  5. Increase uniqueness and expertise signals.
- **Example**: Ethan contrasts high-value content with the common pattern of nonexperts using content tools to rewrite each other’s articles into generic pages.

## Core Advice
- **When you think AEO is totally separate from SEO**: start with SEO fundamentals, then layer on citation optimization and long-tail question coverage — because AEO is still search plus summarization.
- **When you are early-stage and lack domain authority**: prioritize AEO citation optimization before heavy classic SEO — because you can appear in LLM answers quickly through Reddit, YouTube, blogs, and other citations.
- **When you do not know which prompts matter**: start from paid search terms, competitor bidding terms, and money keywords — because they already reflect commercial intent.
- **When keyword tools are incomplete**: mine sales calls, support tickets, Reddit, and customer conversations — because many valuable prompts never show up in traditional tools.
- **When you want offsite visibility**: build presence in YouTube, Reddit, affiliates, blogs, and category publishers — because LLMs synthesize multiple trusted citations.
- **When you are tempted to spam Reddit**: use real employee accounts, disclose affiliation, and be useful — because authentic participation survives moderation and builds trust.
- **When resources are limited**: start with a few high-quality Reddit comments and niche YouTube videos — because a small number of strong citations can matter.
- **When building AEO landing pages**: answer the main question plus follow-ups, subtopics, use cases, integrations, and edge cases — because comprehensive pages are more likely to be cited.
- **When attribution is unclear**: use answer-tracking tools and post-conversion questions — because LLM influence often gets misattributed to branded search or direct traffic.
- **When best practices conflict**: run controlled experiments with test and control groups — because much published advice is untested.
- **When using AI for content**: use AI as an assistant, not a full autopilot — because fully automated content tends to underperform.
- **When deciding whether to block LLMs**: allow indexing for visibility, but block training bots separately if needed — because blocking indexing removes you from the answer surface.

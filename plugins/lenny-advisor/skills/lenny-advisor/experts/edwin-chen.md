## Bio

His perspective matters because he combines deep technical experience from Google, Facebook, and Twitter with a rare obsession for quality, measurement, and building durable companies outside the usual Silicon Valley hype cycle.

He is especially valuable to seek out when you need a sharp view on AI data quality, model evaluation, RL environments, and startup strategy that prioritizes substance over optics.

## Signature Frameworks

### Quality-first AI data design
- **When to use**: When training or evaluating frontier AI models where superficial correctness is not enough and nuanced human judgment matters.
- **Steps**:
  1. Define quality beyond checkbox compliance and explicit instructions.
  2. Identify subtle, domain-specific attributes that distinguish excellent outputs from merely acceptable ones.
  3. Collect thousands of signals on contributors, tasks, and outputs.
  4. Filter out the worst-quality work while separately discovering the best contributors.
  5. Use model performance improvements and expert review loops to validate whether the data actually improves downstream behavior.
- **Example**: A mediocre poem may satisfy “eight lines” and “mentions the moon,” but a high-quality poem uses imagery, surprise, emotion, and insight into moonlight.

### Human expert evaluation over benchmark chasing
- **When to use**: When you need to measure real model progress rather than optimize for public leaderboards or academic benchmarks.
- **Steps**:
  1. Recruit expert evaluators at the top of their fields.
  2. Have them interact with models on realistic tasks in their domain.
  3. Require deep verification of outputs rather than quick preference judgments.
  4. Score models on dimensions like accuracy, instruction following, usefulness, and domain correctness.
  5. Use these evaluations to compare checkpoints and guide training priorities.
- **Example**: Physicists, teachers, and coders have real conversations with models and verify equations, lesson plans, and code instead of relying on casual A/B votes.

### Reinforcement learning environments for real-world capability
- **When to use**: When models perform well on isolated tasks but fail on messy, multi-step, end-to-end workflows.
- **Steps**:
  1. Build a simulated environment that mirrors a real workflow, tools, and artifacts.
  2. Give the model a concrete goal inside that environment.
  3. Let the model act over long horizons using tools and intermediate decisions.
  4. Define rewards based on real outcomes, tests, or artifacts produced.
  5. Inspect not just final success but the trajectory the model took to get there.
- **Example**: A model works inside an environment with Gmail, Slack, Jira, GitHub, and a codebase, then diagnoses and fixes an AWS outage.

### Build the company only you could build
- **When to use**: When deciding what startup to start or how to stay focused amid pressure to pivot, fundraise, or copy Silicon Valley norms.
- **Steps**:
  1. Start from a unique insight rooted in your own background and obsessions.
  2. Commit to a mission rather than chasing whatever category is hot.
  3. Say no to pivots that dilute the original insight.
  4. Keep the team small and highly aligned around quality and craft.
  5. Let product excellence and word of mouth drive adoption instead of hype.
- **Example**: Surge was built from Chen’s frustration with poor training data at big tech companies and his belief that advanced AI would require a new kind of data company.

## Core Advice
- **When you face pressure to grow by fundraising and posting constantly**: stay heads down, build a clearly superior product, and rely on mission-aligned customers and word of mouth — because hype can distract from product quality and attract the wrong users.
- **When an AI training problem seems solvable by adding more labor**: define quality precisely and instrument rich signals about contributors and outputs — because quality is subjective, domain-specific, and not solved by scale alone.
- **When public benchmarks suggest one model is best**: run your own expert-led human evaluations on realistic tasks — because benchmarks can be gamed, leak, or miss real-world usefulness.
- **When designing an AI assistant’s behavior**: optimize for the user’s time, truth, and long-term productivity — because engagement incentives often produce flattery, verbosity, and distraction.
- **When tempted to pivot repeatedly**: stay committed to a deep mission only you are uniquely suited to pursue — because important companies come from conviction, not trend-chasing.
- **When models score well on benchmarks but fail in workflows**: build RL environments that simulate real tools, ambiguity, and long-horizon tasks — because this reveals failure modes hidden by single-step tests.
- **When hiring for advanced AI data or research work**: hire people who love qualitative inspection of model behavior — because progress depends on taste and close analysis, not just credentials.
- **When tempted to adopt vibe coding broadly in production**: be cautious and prioritize maintainability — because short-term speed can create long-term codebase debt.
